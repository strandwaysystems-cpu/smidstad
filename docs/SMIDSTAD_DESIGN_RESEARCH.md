## 1. Site Structure & Pages by Sub-Audience

### 1A. Fitness Coaches & Personal Trainers

**Audience reality:** Digitally-native, often found through Instagram/TikTok. Potential clients arrive warm (they've seen content), skeptical (many coaches exist), and mobile-first. The site's job is to convert a curious follower into a booked consult or sold program within 60 seconds.

#### Recommended Page Order

| # | Page/Section | Purpose | Must-Have Elements |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Homepage** | Convert first-time visitor; communicate transformation | Hero (trainer in action, clear headline, CTA), credibility bar, services preview, 2–3 testimonials, lead magnet opt-in |
| 2 | **Services / Programs** | Sell the offer | Program names, benefit-driven descriptions, pricing (or "starting at"), individual CTAs, transformation photos |
| 3 | **Transformations / Results** | Build belief | Before/after gallery, client story thumbnails, timeline-style results (Week 0 → Week 12), video testimonials |
| 4 | **About** | Build personal trust | Trainer's story + credentials (REPs/NASM/ACE), photo in action, personal hook that mirrors client pain |
| 5 | **Book / Free Consult** | Primary conversion | Embedded booking calendar (Acuity/Calendly), intake form, clear value statement for why the call is free |
| 6 | **Testimonials / Social Proof** | Remove objections | Named testimonials with photos + specific results, Google review widget, Instagram feed embed |
| 7 | **Blog / Resources** *(optional)* | SEO + authority | Fitness tips, content that mirrors social posts |
| 8 | **Contact** | Fallback path | Simple form, social links, email |

**Must-have features (beyond pages):**
- **Booking calendar** embedded on multiple pages (not just a contact form) — [Squarespace Scheduling](https://www.squarespace.com/templates), [Acuity](https://acuityscheduling.com), or Calendly integration
- **Payment/package checkout** — Squarespace Commerce or Stripe integration for selling session packages and programs
- **Before/after transformation gallery** — slider or timeline format (the [Bailard Squarespace template](https://www.squareko.com/website-and-seo/best-squarespace-templates-for-personal-trainers-and-fitness-studios-in-2026) is specifically built for this)
- **Free lead magnet** — workout plan PDF, free week trial, or fitness assessment. Per [9lever.com](https://9lever.com/blog/why-your-coaching-funnel-isnt-converting), lead magnets convert 3–6× better than cold "book a call" CTAs
- **Client portal link** (optional but growing) — Trainerize, TrueCoach, or custom member area
- **Social feed embed** — mirrors the trainer's IG/TikTok for freshness and social proof simultaneously

**Credential signals that matter:** REPs, NASM, ACE, CIMSPA, Level 4 PT — display prominently near hero or in credibility bar. Per [Squareko's 2026 template analysis](https://www.squareko.com/website-and-seo/best-squarespace-templates-for-personal-trainers-and-fitness-studios-in-2026), trainer credentials shown with "trust badges" reduce buyer hesitation significantly.

### 1B. Online Coaches (Life, Business, Wellness)

**Audience reality:** These clients are buying a transformation — not a service. They need to feel understood, see proof that the coach delivers results, and take a low-friction first step. The application/discovery-call funnel is the standard model. Unlike fitness coaches, online coaches often sell higher-ticket programs ($1,500–$10,000+), which means the site must do more pre-selling before the CTA.

#### Key Differences from Fitness Coaches

| Element | Fitness Coach | Online Coach |
|---|---|---|
| Primary conversion goal | Book a free consult or buy a program | Apply for a program / book discovery call |
| Offer clarity needed | Medium (programs are tangible) | High (transformation is intangible) |
| Social proof type | Before/after photos, weight lost | Outcome statements ("tripled revenue," "left toxic job") |
| Funnel complexity | Simple (follow → site → book) | More layered (follow → lead magnet → email → apply) |
| Pricing display | Often shown or "from $X" | Often hidden; "investment on application" |
| Trust signal priority | Physical credentials, body transformations | Story, authority, media mentions, client case studies |

#### Recommended Page Order

| # | Page/Section | Purpose | Must-Have Elements |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Homepage** | Pre-sell before the call | Pain-led headline (client language), credibility bar (media logos, metrics), transformation section, how-it-works (3 steps), social proof, lead magnet opt-in, final CTA |
| 2 | **Work With Me / Services** | Clarify the offer | 1–3 core programs max, each with: outcome-focused description, who it's for, what's included, CTA to apply or book |
| 3 | **About / My Story** | Authority + relatability | Origin story (why the coach does this), credentials, media mentions, personal photo, mission statement, CTA |
| 4 | **Apply / Book a Call** | Primary conversion | Qualifying application form (3–5 questions: current situation, problem, what they've tried, timeline, investment level), then booking link |
| 5 | **Testimonials / Case Studies** | Remove final objections | Named testimonials with photos, specific outcome metrics, video testimonials |
| 6 | **Lead Magnet / Freebie** | Cold traffic capture | Free PDF, quiz, diagnostic, or mini-training with email opt-in |
| 7 | **Blog / Podcast / Resources** *(optional)* | Authority + SEO | Content hub that demonstrates expertise |
| 8 | **Contact** | Fallback | Simple form, clear email |

**The application funnel is critical.** Per [The Revenue Architect](https://9lever.com/blog/why-your-coaching-funnel-isnt-converting): coaches who add a qualifying questionnaire between form fill and booking see close rates double within 30 days. Questions should cover: revenue/situation level, primary problem, what they've tried, timeline. This filters out non-buyers before consuming calendar time.

**Funnel temperature segmentation:**
- Cold traffic (from ads, Google): Send to lead magnet first. Lead magnet converts 3–6× better than a cold call CTA.
- Warm traffic (email list, social followers): Send directly to "Apply Now" or "Book a Discovery Call."

**Offer clarity rule:** Each program page must answer within 5 seconds: *Who is this for? What outcome will I get? What's the investment? What's the first step?* Per [warmsoulgallery.com](https://warmsoulgallery.com/2025/07/30/the-ultimate-checklist-what-every-business-coach-course-creator-website-needs-to-convert-clients/), "feature-heavy" service descriptions kill conversion; benefit-driven copy (focused on outcomes, not deliverables) is the fix.

**Sub-niche flavor differences:**
- **Life/mindset coaches:** Lead with story, vulnerability, and personal transformation. Warm, inviting design. Gabby Bernstein ([gabbyb.tv](https://gabbyb.tv)) exemplifies the "spiritual teacher + membership funnel" model.
- **Business coaches:** Lead with authority, credentials, and revenue outcomes. Clean, professional, CEO-level design. Marie Forleo ([marieforleo.com](https://www.marieforleo.com)) balances personality with business credibility. Michael Hyatt ([michaelhyatt.com](https://michaelhyatt.com)) shows a productized, system-led approach.
- **Wellness coaches:** Lead with empathy and earthiness. Avoid clinical coldness and avoid overly "woo" aesthetics. The [Wilderness Within case study](https://tuluastudio.com/2025/12/11/squarespace-webdesign-for-wellness-coach-wilderness-within/) shows earthy, layered, intentional design that communicates grounded calm.

### 1C. Influencers & Content Creators

**Audience reality:** Creators operate across multiple touchpoints simultaneously — their IG bio, their TikTok, their YouTube, brand DMs, and email. Their web presence needs to serve two very different audiences: (a) their followers/fans, and (b) brands looking to collaborate. These needs are often in tension and must be architectured separately.

#### Link-in-Bio vs. Full Site vs. Media Kit — When Each Is Needed

| Tool | Best For | When to Use It | When NOT to Rely on It Alone |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Link-in-Bio page** (Linktree, Beacons, Hopp, Koji) | Capturing social traffic, campaign launches, product drops, daily updates | Early-stage creators; single main offer; social-first discovery; mobile-only audience | When you need SEO, multiple service pages, owned email list infrastructure, or press/media depth |
| **Full website** | SEO, brand deals credibility, long-form content, owned audience, e-commerce | When pitching brands; selling digital products; publishing a blog/podcast hub; scaling beyond social | As the only web touchpoint without a link-in-bio — most social traffic needs a dedicated mobile landing page |
| **Media kit** (PDF or web page) | Pitching brand collaborations | Any creator seeking paid sponsorships or brand deals | As a substitute for a full website — it's a pitch document, not a discovery tool |

Per [Hopp by Wix](https://www.hopp.co/post/why-link-in-bio), 94% of TikTok traffic and 91% of Instagram traffic comes from mobile. In 2025, the link-in-bio is increasingly treated as "the new homepage" for creators — but it's optimized for *action*, not *depth*. The winning model is **both**: a brand-stylized link-in-bio for social traffic + a full site for SEO, press, and brand deal credibility.

#### Recommended Full Site Page Order (Established Creator)

| # | Page/Section | Purpose | Must-Have Elements |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Homepage** | Brand hub; fan + brand welcome | Big personality hero image/video, brief bio, featured content, top monetization links (shop, course, affiliate storefronts), social embeds, newsletter opt-in |
| 2 | **About / My Story** | Build connection | Authentic narrative, niche positioning, credibility milestones (follower counts, notable brands, features) |
| 3 | **Work With Me / Collaborations** | Convert brand deal inquiries | Media kit download or embed, past brand partnerships (with names/logos), audience stats, content formats offered, rates (or "request rates"), inquiry form |
| 4 | **Shop / Products** | Direct monetization | Digital products, merch, courses, presets, ebooks |
| 5 | **Content Hub / Blog** | SEO + affiliate traffic | Blog posts, YouTube embeds, affiliate link integration (LTK, ShopMy, Amazon Storefront) |
| 6 | **Press / Features** *(optional)* | Authority for brand deals | Media mentions, podcast appearances, publication features |
| 7 | **Contact** | Brand inquiry fallback | Short form, collaboration email, management contact if applicable |

#### Media Kit Contents (What Brands Actually Need)

Based on [Google for Creators guidelines](https://creators.google/hi-in/content-creation-guides/content-monetization-tools/create-a-media-kit-to-secure-brand-deals/), a complete media kit includes:

| Section | What to Include |
|---|---|
| **Bio + brand summary** | Photo, 2–3 sentence bio, niche, values/aesthetic, audience persona description |
| **Platform stats** (lead with strongest) | Follower counts, 30-day avg impressions, story views, Reels/TikTok views, engagement rate |
| **Audience demographics** | Age range, gender split, top locations, income (if available via analytics) |
| **Past brand partnerships** | Brand logos, collaboration type (sponsored post, UGC, long-term ambassador), content examples |
| **Content formats offered** | Static posts, Reels/TikTok, Stories, YouTube, blog, UGC-only, whitelisting |
| **Rates** | Per-post, per-campaign, package rates — or "request a rate card" |
| **Campaign ideas** | 2–3 creative concepts showing how the creator would feature a brand |
| **Contact / CTA** | Dedicated collaboration email, booking form, or management contact |

**Format:** PDF with clickable links is standard (per Google for Creators). Some creators now host a "Collabs" or "Work With Me" page on their site that functions as an interactive media kit.

#### Creator Monetization — How the Site Supports Each Stream

| Revenue stream | How the site enables it |
|---|---|
| **Brand deals / sponsorships** | Work With Me page + media kit PDF + inquiry form |
| **Affiliate links** (LTK, ShopMy, Amazon) | "Favorites" or "Shop" page; blog posts with embedded affiliate links; link-in-bio storefront |
| **Digital products** (presets, templates, ebooks) | Shop page with Gumroad/Shopify/Payhip integration |
| **Courses / memberships** | Course sales page (or Kajabi/Teachable link); email list built via lead magnet |
| **Community / newsletter** | Prominent opt-in across all pages; "Start here" path for new fans |
| **UGC licensing** | "Work With Me" page section on UGC-only packages |

## 2. Design Styles & Aesthetics (2025–2026)

### 2A. Overall Trends Relevant to This Audience

The 2025–2026 design landscape has moved away from pure minimalism toward what [AKNova Studio](https://www.aknovastudio.com/news/web-design-trends-2025) calls "**minimalist maximalism**" — maximum visual impact from a small number of elements. Key trends for creator/coach personal brands:

| Trend | What It Looks Like | Relevance to Creator/Coach Audience |
|---|---|---|
| **Bold/oversized typography** | 60px+ headlines, type as the hero element, kinetic lettering | High — communicates confidence and makes a strong first impression on mobile; [Figma's 2026 trends report](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/web-design-trends/) cites this as central for lifestyle and beauty brands |
| **Narrative/scroll design** | Each section tells a story; animated scroll transitions | High — coaches sell transformation, so storytelling layout directly supports conversion |
| **Mobile-first vertical layout** | Designed for phone-first, thumb-friendly buttons, short copy blocks | Essential — 91–94% of IG/TikTok traffic is mobile ([LinkDash 2026](https://linkdash.eu/en/blog/mobile-first-bio-link-design-12-point-checklist-for-creators-in-2026)) |
| **Vibrant dopamine-color palettes** | Bright saturated hues, neon gradients, Y2K influences | Relevant for fitness, beauty/lifestyle, and creator niches — especially youth-skewing brands |
| **Dark mode** | Black-based layouts with high contrast | Relevant for fitness/performance brands, beauty, and edgy lifestyle creators; now a standard expectation per [Figma](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/web-design-trends/) |
| **Micro-animations** | Subtle hover effects, button ripples, scroll-triggered fades | High — creates a premium, polished feel without distracting from conversion |
| **Authentic photography** | Real photos of the coach/creator (not stock) | Non-negotiable — per [lovepixelagency.com](https://lovepixelagency.com/personal-brand-website-examples/), visitors sense stock photography and it kills trust |
| **Glassmorphism** | Frosted glass panels, depth effects | Medium — works for beauty, wellness, and premium lifestyle feels |
| **Editorial/scrapbook layouts** | Magazine-style grids, layered imagery, collage elements | High for lifestyle/beauty creators; the [Redwood template from Pixpa](https://www.pixpa.com/templates/details/redwood) and editorial Showit templates use this heavily |
| **Video backgrounds** | Short looping hero video of trainer/creator in action | High for fitness and content creators — per [Betheme Blog 2026](https://muffingroup.com/blog/personal-trainer-websites/), the strongest fitness homepages use either a static high-quality photo or short looping video |

### 2B. Aesthetic Differences by Sub-Niche

| Sub-Niche | Visual Language | Colors | Typography | Photography Style | Layout Feel |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Fitness coaches / personal trainers** | Bold, energetic, photo-driven, high-contrast | Black + white + one vibrant accent (electric blue, bold orange, hot pink, deep teal); sometimes full-black or dark palette | Extra-large, confident sans-serifs; bold headline weight; minimal decorative type | Trainer in action, dynamic movement shots, transformation photos, real clients | Grid-based, full-bleed images, strong CTA buttons throughout; [BearWolf](https://www.founderjar.com/inspiration/fitness-websites/) exemplifies all-black high-contrast |
| **Wellness coaches** | Soft, earthy, grounded, calm | Warm neutrals: terracotta, sage, stone, muted greens, warm whites, linen; occasional deep forest greens | Elegant serifs for headlines paired with simple body text; generous spacing | Natural light, outdoor settings, coach in nature or quiet studio, textures | Layered but uncluttered; stepped sections that guide calmly; [Wilderness Within case study](https://tuluastudio.com/2025/12/11/squarespace-webdesign-for-wellness-coach-wilderness-within/) is the archetype |
| **Business coaches** | Clean, authoritative, CEO-level | Navy, deep blue, charcoal + white; gold or teal accent for premium feel; muted color palettes | Strong serif for headlines (Playfair Display-style), clean Open Sans body; hierarchy-driven | Professional headshots, workspace shots, speaking/stage photos, no candid-casual | Clean grid; generous whitespace signals confidence; per [web4business.com.au](https://www.web4business.com.au/portfolio-item/website-design-for-coaches-consultants-the-ultimate-guide/), "less is often more" for authority |
| **Life / mindset coaches** | Warm, story-driven, inclusive | Warm pinks, soft lavenders, creams, earthy neutrals; Gabby Bernstein-style soft gradients | Mix of friendly serif and soft sans-serif; approachable rather than stiff | Authentic lifestyle photos, coach in relatable settings, community/group shots | Section-by-section story narrative; emotional design — feels like a conversation |
| **Beauty / lifestyle creators** | Editorial, elegant, magazine-quality | Black + white + one fashion accent (blush, champagne, rust); OR bold dopamine brights for Gen Z-skewing brands | Elegant editorial serifs for headers; clean modern body text; high-contrast | Polished editorial photography, styled flat-lays, model-quality beauty shots | Magazine grid layouts; the [Redwood Pixpa template](https://www.pixpa.com/templates/details/redwood) shows the editorial-journal approach |
| **Fitness / lifestyle influencers** | Personal-brand forward, aspirational, bright | Platform-native: bright, Instagram-worthy palettes; often the creator's own "signature color" | Bold display fonts; personality-driven; often large and punchy on mobile | Creator's face prominently in hero; lifestyle aesthetic matching IG feed; high-production | Content hub feel; product/affiliate links prominent; Wix and Squarespace templates built for this |

### 2C. Typography Guidance

| Recommendation | Details |
|---|---|
| **Headlines** | Bold, large (60px+ desktop), benefit-driven. One strong serif OR a confident sans-serif. Avoid anything generic or "website-default." |
| **Body text** | Clean, readable, minimum 16px. Open Sans, Inter, DM Sans, or Lato. Not italic for body copy. |
| **Pairing** | One headline font + one body font. Maximum 2 font families. |
| **For fitness/performance** | Condensed or extended sans-serifs (e.g., Bebas Neue, Oswald, or similar). Feel: powerful, direct. |
| **For wellness/feminine brands** | Elegant serif headline (Cormorant Garamond, Libre Baskerville) + soft sans body. Feel: warm, trustworthy. |
| **For business/authority coaches** | Playfair Display or high-contrast serif for headlines + Open Sans. Feel: premium, intelligent. |
| **For beauty/editorial** | Fashion-magazine serifs (Bodoni-style) + minimal sans. Feel: elevated, luxury. |
| **Variable fonts** | 2026 trend per [Figma](https://www.figma.com/resource-library/web-design-trends/) — respond to device size for better mobile display. |

### 2D. Mobile-First Design — The Non-Negotiable

Per [LinkDash's 2026 mobile-first checklist](https://linkdash.eu/en/blog/mobile-first-bio-link-design-12-point-checklist-for-creators-in-2026):
- **94% of TikTok traffic and 91% of Instagram traffic is mobile**
- Creators who switch to mobile-first design see **34% more clicks on key links**
- Tap targets must be minimum **48px** — undersize buttons alone can drop click rate 10–15%
- Page load target: **under 2.5 seconds** (Google standard); real-world 2025 benchmark is 1.5s or less
- Test in **Instagram and TikTok in-app browsers**, not just Chrome — they render differently

Design principles for this audience's mobile reality:
- Design for the smallest screen **first**, scale up
- Short copy blocks (2–3 lines max between subheadings)
- CTAs visible without scrolling (above the fold on mobile)
- Hamburger/minimal nav; 5 items maximum
- Images compressed; no autoplay video on mobile (or use lightweight looping mp4)

## 3. Conversion & UX Patterns

### 3A. Above-the-Fold Formula

The hero section is the most critical real estate. Per multiple sources including [Fjōr Avenue](https://www.fjoravenue.com/en-us/blogs/showit-website-templates-for-coaches-photographers-and-designers/the-best-coaching-website-design-what-actually-works-in-2025) and [Nora Kramer Designs](https://norakramerdesigns.com/best-website-design-practices-for-coaches-and-consultants/), within 3–5 seconds a visitor must answer:

1. **Who are you?** — Name + face visible
2. **What do you do?** — Clear value statement, not vague
3. **Who do you help?** — Named target ("online fitness coaches," "ambitious women entrepreneurs")
4. **What's the first step?** — One primary CTA button

**Hero formula that converts:**
```
[Full-width action photo or looping video of you]
Headline: [Transformation or outcome — NOT "Welcome to my website"]
Subheadline: [Who you help + how]
CTA button: [Action-oriented — "Book a Free Call" / "Start Your Transformation" / "Apply Now"]
```

Per [The Revenue Architect](https://9lever.com/blog/why-your-coaching-funnel-isnt-converting): "The reader doesn't care what you do. They care whether you understand what's wrong with them." The hero headline must name the client's pain in *their* language before introducing the coach's methodology.

### 3B. Homepage Section Order (Proven Pattern)

From synthesis across [Fjōr Avenue](https://www.fjoravenue.com/en-us/blogs/showit-website-templates-for-coaches-photographers-and-designers/the-best-coaching-website-design-what-actually-works-in-2025), [Lovepixel Agency](https://lovepixelagency.com/personal-brand-website-examples/), and [warmsoulgallery.com](https://warmsoulgallery.com/2025/07/30/the-ultimate-checklist-what-every-business-coach-course-creator-website-needs-to-convert-clients/):

1. **Hero** — Headline + subheadline + CTA + professional photo
2. **Credibility bar** — "As seen in" logos, certification badges, key metrics ("500+ clients," "Featured in Forbes")
3. **Problem statement** — Mirror the client's pain in their language
4. **Transformation section** — Before/after, outcomes, what life looks like after working with you
5. **How it works** — Simple 3-step process; reduces friction
6. **Social proof** — 2–3 strong testimonials with names, photos, and specific results
7. **About teaser** — Brief personal story with link to full About page
8. **Services overview** — 1–3 core offers with individual CTAs
9. **Lead magnet opt-in** — Free resource with email capture form
10. **Final CTA** — Repeat primary call to action

### 3C. CTA Patterns That Work

| Pattern | When to Use | Example copy |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary CTA (warm traffic)** | Social followers, email list | "Book Your Free Call" / "Apply Now" / "Start Your Transformation" |
| **Lead magnet CTA (cold traffic)** | Ads, Google, discovery | "Get the Free [X] Guide" / "Take the Quiz" / "Download Your Free Workout Plan" |
| **Urgency CTA** | Time-limited offers | "Limited Spots Available — Apply Now" |
| **Application CTA (high-ticket)** | $2K+ offers | "Apply to Work With Me" / "Join the Waitlist" |
| **Soft CTA (skeptical visitor)** | First touchpoint | "Learn More" / "See How It Works" |

**CTA placement:** Every 2–3 scroll sections per [warmsoulgallery.com](https://warmsoulgallery.com/2025/07/30/the-ultimate-checklist-what-every-business-coach-course-creator-website-needs-to-convert-clients/). Include in: hero, after services, after testimonials, end of page, footer. Include in navigation bar as a button (contrasting color).

**Button design rules:**
- Contrasting color that pops against background (not a same-family color)
- Minimum 48px tap height for mobile
- Action-oriented text (verb first: "Book," "Start," "Get," "Apply")
- One primary CTA per page — don't compete with yourself

### 3D. Social Proof Placement

Social proof must be **distributed throughout the site**, not siloed on one page. Research from [official website designers](https://www.officialwebsitedesigners.com/post/professional-web-design-tips-for-coaches-and-consultants) and [Nora Kramer Designs](https://norakramerdesigns.com/best-website-design-practices-for-coaches-and-consultants/) confirms trust is built through "repetition and subtle reinforcement."

**Placement map:**
- **Homepage mid-section** — 2–3 strong testimonials after the transformation section
- **Below each service/program description** — 1 relevant testimonial per offer
- **Dedicated testimonials page** — named clients with photos, video testimonials preferred
- **Near every CTA** — a short pull-quote immediately above or below the CTA button
- **Credibility bar (above the fold)** — logos, metrics, media mentions

**What makes a testimonial convert:**
- Full name (not "J.S. from California") + headshot
- Specific outcome metric ("Lost 22 lbs in 10 weeks," "Replaced my 9-5 income in 3 months")
- Video testimonials outperform written ones — use where possible
- Screenshots of texts/DMs from clients work well for authenticity

### 3E. Lead Magnets — What Works for Each Sub-Audience

| Sub-audience | High-converting lead magnet types |
|---|---|
| **Fitness coaches** | Free workout plan PDF; 7-day challenge; free fitness assessment; TDEE/macro calculator |
| **Online coaches (life/business)** | Quiz ("What's your coaching archetype?"); scorecard/diagnostic ("Score your [X]"); free training video; private podcast episode |
| **Wellness coaches** | Free meditation/breathwork audio; wellness assessment; "7-day reset" guide |
| **Creators (for audience growth)** | Free UGC template pack; preset bundle; content calendar; "starter kit" |
| **Creators (for brand deal pitching)** | Media kit template; rate card; brand pitch email swipe file |

Per [The Revenue Architect](https://9lever.com/blog/why-your-coaching-funnel-isnt-converting), lead magnets convert 3–6× better than cold "book a call" CTAs for cold traffic. **The lead magnet is the front door of the coaching funnel, not the house.**

### 3F. Use of Video

Video is a powerful trust accelerator for this audience — but it must be used strategically:

| Video type | Where it works best | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Hero background video loop** | Fitness coaches, active creators | Short (6–15s), silent loop, MP4 format; compression critical for mobile load speed |
| **"About me" personal story video** | All coaches | 60–90 seconds; personal, direct-to-camera, authentic over polished |
| **Video testimonials** | High-ticket coaches | Most powerful social proof element; even simple iPhone recordings work |
| **Program/offer explainer video** | Business coaches, course creators | 2–3 minutes; shows the value before asking for the sale |
| **Social media feed embed** | All creators/coaches | Fresh, free social proof; shows the brand is alive and posting |

### 3G. Common Mistakes That Kill Conversion

Compiled from [The Revenue Architect](https://9lever.com/blog/why-your-coaching-funnel-isnt-converting), [warmsoulgallery.com](https://warmsoulgallery.com/2025/07/30/the-ultimate-checklist-what-every-business-coach-course-creator-website-needs-to-convert-clients/), [lovepixelagency.com](https://lovepixelagency.com/personal-brand-website-examples/), and [Nora Kramer Designs](https://norakramerdesigns.com/best-website-design-practices-for-coaches-and-consultants/):

1. **Hero section talks about the coach, not the client.** Opening with "Welcome, I'm [Name] and I'm passionate about…" is a conversion killer. Lead with the client's problem.
2. **Vague transformation.** "I help people live their best life" says nothing. "I help online fitness coaches hit $10K/month without burning out" says everything.
3. **No lead magnet for cold traffic.** Asking a cold visitor to "book a 30-minute call" is asking for marriage on the first date.
4. **Social proof on one page only.** Trust must be distributed; hiding testimonials on a single page means most visitors never see them.
5. **Stock photos.** Visitors sense them immediately and they erode the authenticity that creator/coach brands depend on.
6. **Slow load speed.** Each additional second drops conversion rates by 4.42% ([lovepixelagency.com](https://lovepixelagency.com/personal-brand-website-examples/)). Coaching/creator sites are often image-heavy; uncompressed files are a silent conversion killer.
7. **Too many navigation items.** More than 5–6 nav items creates decision paralysis.
8. **Desktop-designed site deployed to mobile traffic.** 91–94% of traffic from IG/TikTok is mobile. A site designed on a 1440px monitor that isn't tested on phone is broken for most of its visitors.
9. **Prioritizing aesthetics over psychology.** A beautiful site that doesn't answer "what do I do next" every few scrolls loses the sale.
10. **No follow-up sequence.** 80% of coaching conversions happen on touch 5 or later ([The Revenue Architect](https://9lever.com/blog/why-your-coaching-funnel-isnt-converting)). A site with no email capture and no follow-up is leaving most of its leads to die.

## 4. Implications for Mockup Design

### 4A. Do/Don't by Sub-Audience

#### Fitness Coaches & Personal Trainers

| ✅ DO | ❌ DON'T |
|---|---|
| Use full-bleed action photography of the trainer in motion | Use stock fitness photos — they look generic and kill trust |
| High-contrast, bold color palette (black + 1 vibrant accent) | Use pastel or muted colors — this audience reads them as weak/amateur |
| Extra-large typography (60px+) for headlines on mobile | Use small decorative fonts that are illegible on phone |
| Show the transformation story: before/after, timeline (Week 0 → Week 12) | Hide testimonials; put them on one page only |
| Embed a booking calendar directly in the page, not just a "Contact" form | Force visitors to email to inquire — frictionless booking is table stakes |
| Put pricing or "programs from $X" visible (even a range) | Completely hide all pricing — it creates friction and distrust |
| Credentials badge (NASM, ACE, Level 4 PT) in credibility bar | Omit credentials — the fitness space is crowded; credentials differentiate |
| Lead magnet (free workout PDF, free week) for cold traffic | Send all traffic to "Book a Call" with no intermediate step |
| Short looping video background in hero (mobile-friendly) | Use autoplay video with no compression — it bricks mobile load speed |
| CTA buttons in high-contrast accent color, every 2–3 scrolls | Use one CTA buried at the bottom |

#### Online Coaches (Life, Business, Wellness)

| ✅ DO | ❌ DON'T |
|---|---|
| Open hero with client pain statement in *client language* | Open with "Welcome, I'm [Name]…" |
| Separate warm vs. cold traffic CTAs (lead magnet for cold, "Apply" for warm) | Use a single "Book a Call" CTA for all traffic |
| Use a qualifying application form (3–5 questions) before showing the calendar | Let anyone book without qualification — fills calendar with non-buyers |
| Show specific outcome metrics in testimonials ("Tripled revenue," "Left 9-5 in 6 months") | Use vague "She changed my life!" testimonials without concrete outcomes |
| Keep navigation to 5 items max; make "Work With Me" the primary nav CTA | Use a 10-item nav with equal visual weight on everything |
| Sub-niche the aesthetic (earthy for wellness; clean authority for business) | Use a generic template that doesn't signal which world you're in |
| Include a credibility bar with media logos, credentials, or key metrics | Go straight from hero to services with no trust-building midpoint |
| Add email lead magnet opt-in above the fold AND mid-page | Build the site with no email capture — social followers are rented; email is owned |
| About page tells the coach's *transformation story* as a client-facing narrative | Make About page a dry resume/credential list |
| Use 2–3 brand colors consistently; whitespace for premium feel | Use 5+ colors; a visually cluttered palette signals low-end |

#### Influencers & Content Creators

| ✅ DO | ❌ DON'T |
|---|---|
| Optimize the link-in-bio page first — it handles 90%+ of social traffic | Send IG/TikTok bio traffic to a full homepage with nav menu and 8 pages |
| Separate audience-facing links from brand collaboration paths | Mix fan CTAs ("Shop my favorites") with brand CTAs ("Partner with me") at equal hierarchy |
| Build a "Work With Me" page with embedded or linked media kit | Rely on a basic contact form for brand deal inquiries — brands need data |
| Include real audience demographics and platform stats | Leave the Work With Me page vague with no numbers |
| Host the media kit as a downloadable PDF *and* a web page | Media kit as PDF only — some brands want to scan without downloading |
| Showcase 3–5 past brand collaborations with logos and content examples | List brand names only, no visual context |
| Add a "Favorites / Shop" page as the affiliate monetization hub | Scatter affiliate links randomly across pages with no organized hub |
| Build an email list via newsletter/freebie opt-in on every page | Rely entirely on social platforms — algorithm changes kill businesses without owned audiences |
| Use the creator's face prominently in the hero — fans follow *people* | Use an abstract logo or graphic where a face should be |
| Match the website's aesthetic to the creator's Instagram/TikTok visual identity | Build a site that looks disconnected from the social feed — creates brand confusion |

## Summary: The Three Rules That Cut Across All Sub-Audiences

1. **Client-first hero.** The first 5 seconds must name the visitor's problem, not introduce the coach. Every hero headline that starts with "I am…" is a conversion miss.

2. **Mobile is the product.** 91–94% of this audience arrives from Instagram and TikTok on a phone. Every mockup must be designed mobile-first, tested in the Instagram in-app browser, and optimized for sub-2-second load.

3. **Social proof is infrastructure, not decoration.** Testimonials placed on one page are forgettable. Distributed throughout the site — next to every offer, above every CTA, in the hero credibility bar — they become the conversion engine. The difference between a pretty site and a client-getting site is usually this one variable.
